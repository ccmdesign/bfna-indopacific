# Content from Client

## Email

Here are the 6 strait overviews he wrote:

Strait of Malacca — World's busiest waterway, ~40% of global maritime trade. 2 miles wide at narrowest, 82 ft deep at shallowest. Threats: overcrowding, piracy, illicit trade.

Taiwan Strait — ~230 miles N-S, boundary between PRC and Taiwan. ~20% of global maritime trade by value. Threats: geopolitical conflict, blockades.

Strait of Hormuz — Sole connection between Persian Gulf and Indian Ocean. 20M+ barrels/day (2024), ~20% of global petroleum consumption. Threats: geopolitical conflict, state-sponsored seizures, piracy.

Luzon Strait — Separates Taiwan from Philippines, key transpacific link. Japan relies on it for energy imports. Threats: geopolitical conflicts, blockades.

Lombok Strait — Between Lombok and Bali (Indonesia). Deeper than Malacca, increasingly used by largest container ships. Threats: piracy, environmental degradation, illicit trade.

Bab el-Mandeb — Links Indian Ocean to Red Sea/Suez Canal. Key petroleum corridor. Houthi militia attacks on commercial vessels. Threats: piracy, extremist violence, geopolitical conflict.
